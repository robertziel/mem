### Certified Scrum Master — PSM I (Scrum.org) or CSM (Scrum Alliance)

**The entry-level agile/scrum cert.** Required or preferred for many scrum master, tech lead, and engineering manager roles. Two competing bodies — PSM is generally considered harder and more respected in software; CSM is more common in enterprise.

**Two paths:**

| | **PSM I (Scrum.org)** | **CSM (Scrum Alliance)** |
|---|---|---|
| Cost | $200 USD | $700-$1500 USD (mandatory 2-day training) |
| Training | Optional | **Mandatory 2-day course** with certified trainer |
| Exam | 60 min, 80 questions, 85% pass | 60 min, 50 questions, ~74% pass |
| Validity | Lifetime (no renewal) | 2 years (renewal via SEU points + $100) |
| Difficulty | Harder (lower pass rate, ~70-80%) | Easier (95%+ pass after training) |
| Format | Online, unproctored | Online after training |
| Reputation | Respected by engineering | Widely recognized, sometimes viewed as "training certificate" |

**PSM I exam content (based on Scrum Guide 2020):**

| Topic | Emphasis |
|---|---|
| Scrum Framework | Events, artifacts, accountabilities |
| Empiricism (Inspect, Adapt, Transparency) | Core underpinning |
| Scrum values (Commitment, Focus, Openness, Respect, Courage) | Applied in scenarios |
| Coaching and facilitating | Scrum Master role in practice |
| Edge cases and anti-patterns | "What should the Scrum Master do when..." |

**Scrum fundamentals (know cold):**

**3 Roles (Accountabilities in 2020 Scrum Guide):**
- **Product Owner** — maximizes value, owns and orders Product Backlog
- **Scrum Master** — serves the team, removes impediments, teaches Scrum
- **Developers** — build increments, self-managing, cross-functional

**5 Events:**
- **Sprint** — the container; fixed length (1-4 weeks); no goal changes mid-sprint
- **Sprint Planning** — max 8h for 1-month sprint (proportional); produces Sprint Goal + Sprint Backlog
- **Daily Scrum** — 15 min; developers only (SM and PO optional); inspect progress toward Sprint Goal
- **Sprint Review** — max 4h for 1-month sprint; inspect Increment, adapt Product Backlog
- **Sprint Retrospective** — max 3h for 1-month sprint; inspect how the last Sprint went (process, tools, relationships)

**3 Artifacts + Commitments (2020 Scrum Guide):**
- **Product Backlog** → commitment: **Product Goal** (long-term objective)
- **Sprint Backlog** → commitment: **Sprint Goal** (this sprint's focus)
- **Increment** → commitment: **Definition of Done** (quality bar)

**Common exam traps:**
- Daily Scrum is **for developers** (PO and SM may attend but don't run it)
- Sprint length is **fixed at the start** — cannot extend mid-sprint
- Only the **Product Owner** can cancel a Sprint (and only if Sprint Goal becomes obsolete)
- **Sprint Goal cannot be changed** during the Sprint (scope CAN be renegotiated)
- Developers choose **how much** work; PO decides **what** and in **what order**
- Scrum Master is a **true leader** who serves — NOT a project manager, NOT a team lead
- There is no "Scrum Master of the product" or shared Scrum Masters — the Scrum Master belongs to the Scrum Team
- **Impediments** are for the Scrum Master to remove; **obstacles within developer control** are not impediments
- Definition of Done is created by developers (within org standards)
- Scrum is a **framework**, not a methodology — intentionally incomplete

**Anti-patterns that appear on the exam:**
- "What should the Scrum Master do when the PO is pushing mid-sprint scope?" → coach the PO, don't accept scope change
- "Team didn't finish all Sprint Backlog items" → normal; inspect Increment in Review, don't extend Sprint
- "Stakeholder bypasses PO to talk to developers" → SM coaches stakeholder to work through PO
- "Technical debt piling up" → Team negotiates with PO to include in Product Backlog
- "Manager assigns tasks to developers" → SM addresses; developers self-manage

**Extensions worth knowing for advanced questions:**
- Scrum Guide 2020 changes from 2017: "accountabilities" (not "roles"), Product Goal added, simplified language, more inclusive
- Nexus (scaled scrum) — Scrum.org's scaling framework
- LeSS (Large-Scale Scrum) — separate framework
- SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework) — most common in enterprise, but NOT pure scrum

**Study path for PSM I:**
- **Scrum Guide 2020** (18 pages, free, MEMORIZE IT)
- Scrum.org Open Assessments (free, take until you 100% them)
- Volkerdon practice questions (85+ pass consistently predicts real exam pass)
- Mikhail Lapshin's free Scrum quiz
- No course needed — the Scrum Guide + open assessments is enough

**Study path for CSM:**
- Mandatory 2-day course with Certified Scrum Trainer (CST)
- Exam is easy if you attend the course
- Less need for external prep

**Who it's for:** Engineering managers, tech leads, scrum masters (obviously), product managers interested in agile. Many engineer-to-EM promotions ask for scrum credential. Prep time: 10-20 hours for PSM I; 2-day course + minimal prep for CSM.

**Rule of thumb:** Choose **PSM I over CSM** if you're in software engineering — it's cheaper ($200 vs $700+), lifetime valid, and more respected in the engineering community. The Scrum Guide is 18 pages — memorize it. PSM is tricky because wrong answers are plausible but contradict Scrum Guide language; read questions carefully. Avoid anything that smells like "command and control" — scrum is about servant leadership and self-management.

### Navigating Ambiguity & Incomplete Requirements

**Why this is asked:**
- Real-world projects rarely have clear requirements
- Tests: initiative, decision-making, risk management, communication
- Critical for senior+ roles (Google G&L, Amazon Bias for Action)

**Common questions:**
- "Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information."
- "Describe a project where the requirements kept changing."
- "How do you handle situations where there's no clear right answer?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to define the problem, not just solve it."

**Template: Ambiguous project**
```
S: "I was asked to [vague goal: 'improve performance' / 'build a
   recommendation feature' / 'fix the scaling issues']."
T: "The requirements were unclear. No one had defined what success
   looked like or what the constraints were."
A: "I started by [talking to stakeholders / analyzing data / defining
   success metrics]. I identified [3 possible approaches] and wrote
   a one-page proposal comparing them with tradeoffs. I proposed
   starting with [smallest viable approach] to validate assumptions
   before committing to the full solution. I set up [weekly check-ins
   / metrics dashboard] to course-correct early."
R: "[Delivered feature in N weeks / metric improved by X%]. By
   starting small, we avoided [over-engineering / building wrong thing].
   I learned that [defining the problem is half the solution]."
```

**Template: Changing requirements**
```
S: "Mid-project, [stakeholder/business] changed the requirements
   from [A] to [B]."
T: "I needed to assess impact and find a path forward without
   losing the work already done."
A: "I documented the gap between old and new requirements. I identified
   which components could be reused and what needed rebuilding. I
   proposed [phased approach / reduced scope for v1 / parallel track].
   I communicated the timeline impact to stakeholders with options."
R: "[Delivered revised scope on time / only N days delay]. I learned
   to [build with flexibility / invest in interfaces / decouple early]."
```

**Framework for handling ambiguity:**
1. **Clarify** — ask questions, talk to users/stakeholders, define constraints
2. **Scope** — identify the smallest useful first step
3. **Propose** — write up 2-3 options with tradeoffs, recommend one
4. **Validate** — build the smallest thing that tests your assumptions
5. **Iterate** — adjust based on feedback and data

**What interviewers look for:**
| Good signal | Bad signal |
|------------|-----------|
| "I defined success metrics first" | "I just started building" |
| "I proposed options with tradeoffs" | "I waited for someone to tell me what to do" |
| "I started small to validate" | "I spent 3 months planning before building" |
| "I communicated uncertainty to stakeholders" | "I promised a date without understanding scope" |
| "I adapted when new information emerged" | "I stuck with the original plan despite evidence" |

**Rule of thumb:** Show that you DRIVE clarity rather than wait for it. Define the problem, propose options, start small, iterate. The worst answer is "I waited for clearer requirements." The best is "I broke through the ambiguity by doing X."

### Google Googleyness & Leadership Interview

**What Google evaluates:**
Google has a dedicated "Googleyness and Leadership" (G&L) interview round that assesses cultural fit and leadership ability, separate from coding and system design.

**Googleyness dimensions:**
- **Intellectual humility** — can you admit when you're wrong? Do you change your mind with new data?
- **Comfort with ambiguity** — can you make progress when requirements are unclear?
- **Collaborative nature** — do you elevate others? Can you work with diverse viewpoints?
- **Bias toward action** — do you default to doing the right thing, even when it's hard?
- **Conscientiousness** — do you care about doing things well and following through?

**Leadership dimensions (all levels, not just managers):**
- **Navigate ambiguity** — drive clarity in unclear situations
- **Influence without authority** — convince peers through data and reasoning
- **Resolve disagreements** — find alignment while respecting different views
- **Mentor and grow others** — share knowledge, help teammates level up

**Common G&L questions:**

**Ambiguity:**
- "Tell me about a time you had to make a decision with incomplete information."
- "Describe a situation where the requirements kept changing."

**Collaboration:**
- "Tell me about a time you worked with a difficult teammate."
- "How did you resolve a disagreement on a technical approach?"

**Leadership:**
- "Describe a time you led without formal authority."
- "Tell me about when you mentored someone."

**Doing the right thing:**
- "Tell me about a time you pushed back on something you disagreed with."
- "Describe when you prioritized long-term quality over short-term speed."

**What differentiates a "Googley" answer:**
- Show genuine curiosity and learning (not just "I knew the answer")
- Demonstrate you changed your mind when presented with better data
- Show you elevated the team, not just yourself
- Acknowledge contributions of others while showing your specific impact
- Show empathy and user focus

**Scoring (4-point scale):**
| Score | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 1 | Strong No Hire |
| 2 | Lean No Hire |
| 3 | Lean Hire |
| 4 | Strong Hire |

Need 3+ across all interview dimensions. Hiring committee reviews all scores.

**Rule of thumb:** Google values HOW you think over WHAT you achieved. Show intellectual honesty, collaborative problem-solving, and learning from mistakes. The best Googleyness answers show you changed your mind or helped someone else grow.

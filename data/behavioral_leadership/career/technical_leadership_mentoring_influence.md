### Technical Leadership, Mentoring & Influence

**Why this matters:**
- Senior+ roles require leading without formal authority
- Shows you multiply team output, not just your own
- Tested at: Google (G&L), Amazon (Hire and Develop the Best), Meta (initiative)

**Common questions:**
- "Tell me about a time you mentored someone."
- "Describe when you led a technical initiative across teams."
- "How did you influence a technical decision without being the decision-maker?"
- "Tell me about a time you improved your team's engineering practices."

**Template: Mentoring story**
```
S: "A junior engineer on my team was struggling with [specific area]."
T: "I took responsibility for helping them grow in [skill/area]."
A: "I [paired with them on a complex PR / set up weekly 1:1s /
   created a learning plan / gave them ownership of a feature
   with increasing complexity]. I provided feedback by [reviewing
   their code with explanations / walking through design tradeoffs /
   letting them present at design reviews]."
R: "[They shipped feature X independently / got promoted / reduced
   their PR review cycles from 3 days to 1]. I learned that
   [investing in others multiplies team output]."
```

**Template: Technical influence story**
```
S: "Our organization had [technical problem] across multiple teams."
T: "I identified the need for [standard/tool/practice] but had no
   authority over other teams."
A: "I wrote an RFC/proposal documenting the problem with data.
   I built a prototype/proof-of-concept. I presented at [tech talk /
   architecture review / engineering all-hands]. I offered to help
   the first team adopt it and documented the results."
R: "[N teams adopted the approach / saved X hours/week / reduced
   incidents by Y%]. The approach became an organizational standard."
```

**Template: Improving engineering practices**
```
S: "Our team had [poor practice: no tests, slow deploys, no code review]."
T: "I proposed improving [specific practice]."
A: "I didn't mandate changes. Instead, I [led by example / added
   tests to my own PRs / wrote a guide / automated the painful part /
   showed the team the before/after metrics]."
R: "[Test coverage went from 30% to 80% / deploy frequency doubled /
   production incidents dropped 50%]."
```

**Levels of leadership (what's expected at each level):**
| Level | Leadership expectation |
|-------|----------------------|
| Mid (L4) | Mentor one person, improve your team's code quality |
| Senior (L5) | Lead a project end-to-end, influence team practices |
| Staff (L6) | Drive cross-team initiatives, set technical direction |
| Principal (L7) | Org-wide influence, technical strategy, industry impact |

**Key signals interviewers look for:**
- You invested in others' growth (not just your own output)
- You led through influence, not authority
- You left things better than you found them (processes, tools, documentation)
- You can articulate WHY a technical decision matters, not just WHAT

**Rule of thumb:** The best leadership stories show you multiplied team output, not just your own. "I built X" is mid-level. "I enabled the team to build X, Y, and Z" is senior+. Always quantify the impact.

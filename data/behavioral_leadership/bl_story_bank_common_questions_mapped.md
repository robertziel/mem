### Behavioral Story Bank & Question Mapping

**The 8 story categories (prepare at least one each):**

| # | Category | Key questions |
|---|----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Conflict / Disagreement** | Disagreed with manager, teammate conflict, cross-team friction |
| 2 | **Failure / Mistake** | Biggest mistake, production incident, missed deadline |
| 3 | **Leadership / Initiative** | Led a project, mentored someone, drove adoption of a practice |
| 4 | **Ambiguity / Autonomy** | Unclear requirements, made decision with incomplete info |
| 5 | **Achievement / Impact** | Proudest accomplishment, most impactful project |
| 6 | **Collaboration / Teamwork** | Worked across teams, helped a struggling teammate |
| 7 | **Customer / User Focus** | Went above and beyond for user, prioritized user needs |
| 8 | **Growth / Learning** | Learned a new technology quickly, adapted to change |

**Top 20 most asked behavioral questions:**

**Conflict:**
1. Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate on a technical approach.
2. Describe a situation where you had a conflict with your manager.
3. How do you handle disagreements in code review?

**Failure:**
4. Tell me about your biggest professional failure.
5. Describe a time something went wrong in production. What did you do?
6. Tell me about a time you missed a deadline.

**Leadership:**
7. Tell me about a time you led a project end-to-end.
8. Describe when you mentored a junior engineer.
9. How did you improve an engineering process at your company?

**Ambiguity:**
10. Describe a time you had to work with unclear requirements.
11. Tell me about a decision you made with incomplete information.
12. How do you prioritize when everything is urgent?

**Achievement:**
13. What's the most impactful project you've worked on?
14. Tell me about a technical challenge you're proud of solving.
15. Describe a time you delivered something ahead of schedule.

**Collaboration:**
16. Tell me about working with a difficult teammate or stakeholder.
17. Describe a successful cross-team collaboration.
18. How do you handle receiving critical feedback?

**Customer Focus:**
19. Describe a time you advocated for the end user.
20. Tell me about a trade-off between technical debt and user experience.

**Story versatility matrix:**
```
One strong story can answer multiple categories:
"Payment service migration incident"
  -> Failure: what went wrong
  -> Leadership: I led the recovery
  -> Learning: what I changed after
  -> Ambiguity: unclear scope, I defined it

Prepare 8-10 stories that collectively cover all 8 categories.
```

**Preparation checklist:**
- [ ] 8-10 stories written out in STAR format
- [ ] Each story mapped to which questions it can answer
- [ ] Each story mapped to target company's values
- [ ] Quantified results for every story (%, time, money, incidents)
- [ ] Practiced out loud (not just written)
- [ ] Timed each story (2-3 minutes max)
- [ ] Prepared follow-up answers ("What would you do differently?")

**Rule of thumb:** You need 8-10 strong stories, not 20 weak ones. One versatile story can answer 3-4 different questions by emphasizing different aspects. Write them out, practice out loud, and time yourself. The story bank IS the behavioral interview prep.

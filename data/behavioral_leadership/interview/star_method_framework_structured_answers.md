### STAR Method Framework for Behavioral Interviews

**What STAR is:**
- **S**ituation — set the context (where, when, what project/team)
- **T**ask — your specific responsibility or challenge
- **A**ction — what YOU did (use "I", not "we")
- **R**esult — quantified outcome and what you learned

**Timing:** 2-3 minutes per answer. ~30 seconds each for S/T, ~60-90 seconds for A, ~30 seconds for R.

**Good STAR answer structure:**
```
"When I was at [company] working on [project]...        (Situation)
I was responsible for [specific challenge]...            (Task)
I decided to [specific actions, reasoning]...            (Action)
As a result, [measurable outcome]. I learned [lesson]."  (Result)
```

**Example: "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate"**
```
S: "At my previous company, our team was debating whether to
   rewrite our payment service from Ruby to Go."
T: "As the senior engineer, I needed to evaluate the proposal
   and present a recommendation to the team."
A: "I gathered latency data from production, benchmarked both
   implementations, and found that 90% of our latency came from
   database queries, not Ruby's runtime. I presented this data
   in a design review and proposed optimizing our queries and
   adding caching instead."
R: "The team agreed. We reduced p95 latency by 60% in two weeks
   without a rewrite. I learned that data beats opinions in
   technical disagreements."
```

**Common mistakes:**
- Too much context, not enough action (the "A" is 60% of the answer)
- Saying "we" instead of "I" (interviewer wants YOUR contribution)
- No measurable result (always quantify: %, time saved, revenue, incidents prevented)
- Generic answers without specific details
- Not explaining WHY you made decisions

**Preparation strategy:**
- Prepare 8-10 stories that cover multiple categories
- Each story should be versatile (one story can answer conflict + leadership + failure)
- Map stories to company values (Amazon LPs, Google Googleyness)
- Practice out loud (not just in your head)

**Rule of thumb:** The interviewer wants to hear YOUR specific actions and reasoning, not the team's. Quantify results. Every story should end with a lesson learned or growth moment.

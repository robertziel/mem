### Conflict Resolution & Disagreement Stories

**Why this is asked:**
- Most common behavioral category across ALL companies
- Tests: communication, empathy, professional maturity, ability to influence
- Variants: disagreed with manager, teammate conflict, cross-team friction

**Story templates:**

**Template 1: Technical disagreement (resolved with data)**
```
S: "Our team was split on [technology/approach choice]."
T: "I needed to [evaluate/recommend] the right approach for [goal]."
A: "I [gathered data/ran benchmarks/prototyped both]. I presented
   findings in [design review/RFC], showing [specific tradeoff].
   I acknowledged the valid points in the other approach and
   proposed [compromise/hybrid/clear winner with data]."
R: "[Outcome]. The team aligned on [decision]. I learned that
   [data beats opinions / presenting tradeoffs builds trust]."
```

**Template 2: Disagreed with manager (respectful pushback)**
```
S: "My manager wanted to [decision you disagreed with]."
T: "I believed [alternative] was better because [reasoning]."
A: "I scheduled a 1:1, presented [data/examples/risk analysis].
   I framed it as [concern for the project, not personal], asking
   'Have we considered X?' rather than 'You're wrong about Y.'
   [Manager agreed / Manager disagreed and I committed]."
R: "[Outcome]. I learned [how to influence upward / when to disagree
   and commit / how to frame concerns constructively]."
```

**Template 3: Cross-team conflict (alignment building)**
```
S: "Team A needed [X] but Team B's roadmap prioritized [Y]."
T: "I needed to find alignment without authority over either team."
A: "I set up a meeting with both tech leads, mapped dependencies,
   and proposed [compromise / shared timeline / phased approach].
   I documented the agreement and followed up weekly."
R: "[Both teams delivered on time]. I learned [relationships matter /
   written agreements prevent drift / empathy for other team's constraints]."
```

**Key phrases interviewers love:**
- "I sought to understand their perspective first"
- "I presented data rather than opinions"
- "I acknowledged the valid points in their approach"
- "Once the decision was made, I committed fully"
- "I learned that [specific lesson]"

**Key phrases that hurt:**
- "They were clearly wrong" (arrogant)
- "I escalated to their manager" (political, last resort only)
- "I just went along with it" (no backbone)
- "We couldn't agree so we did nothing" (no resolution)

**How to NEVER say you were wrong but show growth:**
- "In retrospect, I would have [gathered more data / involved stakeholders earlier]"
- "I've since developed a framework for [handling similar situations]"

**Rule of thumb:** The interviewer wants to see: you listen first, use data not ego, can disagree respectfully, and commit once decided. The best stories show you changed someone's mind OR someone changed yours.

### Salary Negotiation & Offer Tactics

**When to negotiate:**
- ALWAYS. Companies expect it. Not negotiating leaves money on the table.
- The offer is the starting point, not the final number.
- Negotiate AFTER you have the written offer, not during interviews.

**Preparation:**
- Research comp ranges: levels.fyi, Glassdoor, Blind, Paysa
- Know the components: base salary, signing bonus, equity (RSU/options), annual bonus
- Total compensation (TC) = base + equity/year + bonus/year + signing/year
- Understand your leverage: competing offers, rare skills, market demand

**Negotiation framework:**

**Step 1: Don't give a number first**
```
Recruiter: "What are your salary expectations?"
You: "I'd like to understand the full scope of the role first.
      What's the compensation range for this level?"
```
If pressed: "Based on my research, I'd expect total compensation in
the range of $X-$Y for this level. I'm flexible depending on the
full package."

**Step 2: Receive the offer, ask for time**
```
"Thank you, I'm excited about the opportunity. I'd like a few days
to review the full package. Can I get back to you by [date]?"
```
Always ask for 3-5 business days. Never accept on the spot.

**Step 3: Counter with reasoning**
```
"I'm very excited about [company] and this role. After reviewing the
offer, I'd like to discuss the compensation. Based on [competing offer /
market data / my experience in X], I was hoping for [specific number].
Is there flexibility on [base / equity / signing bonus]?"
```

**What's negotiable:**
| Component | Flexibility | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Base salary | Medium | Often banded by level, limited room |
| Signing bonus | High | One-time cost, easiest to increase |
| Equity (RSUs) | High | Especially at public companies |
| Start date | High | Negotiate gap for rest/transition |
| Level/title | Low-Medium | Harder but highest long-term impact |
| Remote/hybrid | Medium | Increasingly negotiable post-2020 |
| Annual bonus target | Low | Usually fixed by level |

**Competing offers (strongest lever):**
```
"I have an offer from [Company B] at [higher number]. I'd prefer to
join [your company] because [genuine reason]. Can we close the gap
on compensation?"
```
- You don't need to share exact numbers
- Even a verbal offer from another company is leverage
- Never lie about competing offers (recruiters talk)

**Equity negotiation specifics:**
- RSUs: ask about vesting schedule (4-year with 1-year cliff is standard)
- Refreshers: ask about annual equity refreshes (some companies front-load)
- Startups: negotiate for more options + ask about 409A valuation, liquidation preference

**When NOT to push further:**
- Recruiter says "this is the top of band for this level" (ask about leveling up)
- You've gone back and forth 2-3 times (don't over-negotiate and sour the relationship)
- The offer is genuinely competitive and you're happy

**Rule of thumb:** Always negotiate. Counter with data, not emotions. The signing bonus is the easiest lever. Competing offers are the strongest lever. Never accept on the spot. The worst they can say is "this is our best offer" — they won't rescind.
